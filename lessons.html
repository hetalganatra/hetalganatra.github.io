Teen Course...

Students between the ages of 14 years 9 months and 24 years of age are required to complete the teen course in order
to obtain their license. The state requires 32 hours of classroom instruction followed by 14 hours of behind-the-wheel instruction.
During the first week of class at our school, we will administer the written exam. Upon passing, the student will receive
a certificate that allows him or her to trade it in for a permit at the DPS. After obtaining their permit, the student is still
required to complete the remaining hours of class, however they may also begin to schedule their driving lessons outside
of the class time. Once completing all of the classroom and behind-the-wheel instructional hours, the student will then
receive a second certificate, allowing the student to obtain his or her license. In order to obtain a license, the student
must be at least 16 years of age and have held their permit for a minimum of 6 months.

What It Includes...

    ✇  32 Hours of Classroom Instruction
    ✇  14 hours of behind-the-wheel instruction

Requirments...

    ✇  Student must be between the ages of 14 years 9 months and 24 years of age to enroll.
    ✇  Student must have legal status in the U.S.

In order to sign up, the student will need to provide a form of photo ID (preferably passport,if the student does not have a passport
they may bring a state issued ID, or school ID and birth certificate), and a VOE (Verification of Enrollment) from their high school, or
the mailed, official last report card (during summer only)
